Part 1 of 3 · In-Game Excellence
Master
Valorant
🎯
Aim & Mechanics
  • Train on Aim Lab or KovaaK's daily — 30 mins minimum
  • Learn crosshair placement (aim HEAD height, always)
  • Practice counter-strafing (stop moving before shooting)
  • Spray control: learn each gun's recoil pattern
  • Use Valorant's Range every single day before queuing
  • Record your sessions and review your missed shots
🧠
Game Sense & IQ
  • Learn all map layouts: angles, chokepoints, corners
  • Watch your mini-map every 3–5 seconds
  • Communicate: always call enemy positions clearly
  • Learn when to push, when to hold, when to retreat
  • Study enemy patterns mid-game and adapt
  • Play slow — information wins games, not rushing
⚡
Agent & Utility
  • Pick 1–2 agents and master them completely
  • Learn all utility lineups for your main maps
  • Understand when NOT to use your abilities
  • Watch pro players using your agents on YouTube
  • Practice smokes, flashes, and mollies in custom games
  • Learn how to combo abilities with teammates
📈
Rank Climbing
  • Start with 3–5 ranked games per day max (avoid tilt)
  • Review your worst deaths after every session
  • Use tracker.gg to study your stats and weak areas
  • Solo queue early, 5-stack with disciplined players
  • Focus on one mistake to fix per week
  • Never blame teammates — only control what YOU do
🎮
Setup & Hardware
  • Set mouse DPI + sensitivity for consistency (low is better)
  • 144Hz+ monitor makes a real difference — aim for it
  • Wired mouse and keyboard = zero input delay
  • Set Valorant graphics to low for maximum FPS
  • Disable all background apps while playing
  • Good headset = hear footsteps and win more fights
🏅
The Pro Path
  • Target Immortal rank first — then Radiant
  • Join amateur leagues and community tournaments
  • Build a team: find 4 disciplined, coachable players
  • Apply to Valorant Challengers when you reach the level
  • Study VCT matches — how pro teams play angles
  • Consistency beats peak performance: show up every day

Part 2 of 3 · Building Your Channel
Grow Your
YouTube

Starting from zero is normal. Every big gaming YouTuber started with 0 subscribers and 0 views. The secret is simple: upload consistently, improve every video, and serve your audience.

01
Month 1–2
Foundation & First 10 Videos
  • Create your channel and choose a clean name
  • Make a channel banner with your goals and game
  • Write a clear "About" section describing your content
  • Upload your first video — don't wait for perfect quality
  • Post at least 2–3 videos per week
  • Study your first 10 videos and notice what felt wrong
  • Goal: 100 subscribers, just keep uploading
02
Month 3–6
Quality Jump & Consistency
  • Learn basic video editing (CapCut, DaVinci Resolve — free)
  • Add commentary: talk during your highlights
  • Study thumbnails: bright colors, big face reactions, clear text
  • Write better titles using keywords people search for
  • Post on a fixed schedule (example: Tuesday + Friday)
  • Goal: 1,000 subscribers and your first viral clip
03
Month 7–12
Audience & Growth Hacking
  • Reply to EVERY comment in your first year
  • Post Shorts using best clips from your videos
  • Collaborate with other Valorant creators your size
  • Cross-post clips on TikTok, Instagram Reels
  • Watch your YouTube Analytics every week — learn what works
  • Goal: 10,000 subscribers and monetization unlocked
04
Year 2+
Brand, Streams & 1M Target
  • Start live streaming on YouTube alongside videos
  • Build a Discord community for your viewers
  • Create series content: "Road to Radiant", "1v5 Clutches", etc.
  • Partner with small brands (gaming peripherals, energy drinks)
  • Document your esports journey — people LOVE following a story
  • Goal: 100K then relentlessly push toward 1,000,000
01
Content Ideas
Top plays & clutch moments · Rank climbing series · "I went from Iron to Diamond" · Agent guides · Tips for beginners · Reacting to pro plays · 1v5 challenges
02
Tools (Free)
OBS Studio (recording) · CapCut or DaVinci Resolve (editing) · Canva (thumbnails) · TubeBuddy (SEO) · VidIQ (analytics) · Audacity (audio)
03
Thumbnail Rules
High contrast colors · Show your face reacting · Max 4 words of text · Big font visible on mobile · Study thumbnails of channels with 500K+ subscribers
04
Title Formula
Use numbers: "5 tips..." · Use emotion: "insane", "never seen before" · Use keywords people search: "Valorant tips 2025" · Keep under 60 characters
05
Algorithm Tips
Hook viewers in first 10 seconds · Upload on consistent days · Aim for 8–15 min video length · End every video with a "call to action" — like, subscribe, comment
06
What NOT to Do
Never quit after 10 videos · Don't buy subscribers · Don't copy another creator's entire style · Don't stream 8 hours with no editing effort — quality matters

Part 3 of 3 · Your Daily System
The Winning
Routine
MORNING
Aim Training (30 min) Open Aim Lab before anything. Train flicking, tracking, and speed. Don't skip this — aim is a skill that decays fast.
AFTERNOON
Ranked Queue (2–3 hours) Play your ranked games when your mind is fresh. 3–5 games max. Review deaths after each session. Focus on one skill to improve that day.
EVENING
Record & Edit Content (1–2 hours) Clip your best plays, funny moments, or learning moments. Edit a short video or a YouTube Short. Even 5 minutes of content counts.
NIGHT
Study & Research (30–45 min) Watch VCT pro matches. Study how they play your role. Watch top YouTube creators and analyze their thumbnail, title, and first 30 seconds.
ALWAYS
Sleep 7–8 Hours. Drink Water. Take Breaks. Your brain is your most important hardware. Pro gamers take care of their body and mind. No champion was built on 4 hours of sleep.
